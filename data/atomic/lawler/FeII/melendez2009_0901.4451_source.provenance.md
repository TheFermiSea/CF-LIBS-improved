## Data table (inside the .tex, first `table*` environment)
A 142-line Fe II oscillator-strength list, 4000-8000 Angstrom, laid out as a
PRINT-FORMATTED 3-up (triplet) `tabular` -- each printed row carries up to three
(lambda, chi_exc, log gf, C6) blocks separated by `&`. Column meanings:

| Column   | Units       | Meaning                                                     |
|----------|-------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| lambda   | Angstrom    | Wavelength                                                   |
| chi_exc  | eV          | Lower-level excitation potential (NOT a cm^-1 level energy)  |
| log gf   | [-]         | log10(g*f); superscript tags absolute-scale source (see below)|
| C6       | (cgs)       | van der Waals broadening constant; parentheses = uncertain   |

### log gf provenance tags (superscripts on each value)
- `^L` = multiplet absolute scale normalized to LABORATORY gf-values.
- `^S` = multiplet absolute scale normalized to SOLAR gf-values.
Relative gf within a multiplet are from theoretical calculations (Biemont et al.
1991 and related), globally re-normalized per multiplet.

## Parse-check (no transformation applied)
- 142 tagged log gf tokens found (regex `\d.\d{2}\$\^[LS]\$`), matching the
  paper's stated 142 Fe II lines.
- Tag breakdown: 74 lab-normalized (`^L`), 68 solar-normalized (`^S`).
- No per-line uncertainties are provided; no radiative lifetimes (tau); lower
  level given as excitation potential in eV, not as a cm^-1 energy level.

## Build-phase warning
This is a semi-empirical COMPILATION, not a direct laboratory line-by-line
