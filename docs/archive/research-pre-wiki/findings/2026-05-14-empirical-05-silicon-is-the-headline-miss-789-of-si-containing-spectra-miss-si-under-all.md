# Empirical Finding: 78.9% of Si-containing spectra miss Si under all 5 identifiers

## Source

Direct analysis of the n=33 CF-LIBS benchmark export, computed outside of Asta. Not generated by autodiscovery — manually verified.

## Claim

Si is present in 19 spectra; 15 of those miss Si under every identifier (78.9%). The 4 that catch Si: 3 are BHVO-2 (low rp), 1 is Vrabel s039. **Si is the single largest source of recall loss in the entire corpus**, and the failure mode is rp-correlated. This is the highest-leverage residual on the board.

## What to do next

Immediate: look at vrabel_s039 (the one Vrabel spectrum that DOES catch Si) and diff it against the 14 that don't. What's different about s039 — concentration, spectral cleanliness, peak SNR, neighbor-line interference? This is a single-spectrum forensic task and should take <1 hour. The answer is likely worth a dedicated bd issue and may itself unlock 14 of the 53 universal misses.
