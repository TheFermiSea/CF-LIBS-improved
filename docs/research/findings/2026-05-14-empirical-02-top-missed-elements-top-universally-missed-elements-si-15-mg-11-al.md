# Empirical Finding: Top universally-missed elements: Si (15), Mg (11), Al (9), Ti (7) — all major rock-forming

## Source

Direct analysis of the n=33 CF-LIBS benchmark export, computed outside of Asta. Not generated by autodiscovery — manually verified.

## Claim

Of the 53 universally-missed pairs, the element distribution is Si:15, Mg:11, Al:9, Ti:7 (sum 42 of 53). These are not trace elements — they are the *primary rock-forming oxides*. **The 0.69→0.85 macro-F1 gap is dominated by missing majors, not trace/REE.** This directly contradicts the intuitive 'fix the long tail' strategy, and aligns with Asta exp #16 finding that the FN budget is dominated by majors in absolute terms.

## What to do next

Si and Mg should each get a dedicated investigation. The Si investigation is unblocked: see empirical finding #03 below for the rp=30000 confound; the Si lines around 288nm and 252nm are well-characterized and a basis-line audit can probably explain most misses. For Mg, the 285nm doublet self-absorbs at concentrations >10% — partition function fix (PR #168) may have already moved the needle; re-run the benchmark and check.
