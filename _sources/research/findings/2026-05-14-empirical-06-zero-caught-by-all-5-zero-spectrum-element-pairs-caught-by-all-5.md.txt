# Empirical Finding: Zero (spectrum, element) pairs caught by all 5 identifiers

## Source

Direct analysis of the n=33 CF-LIBS benchmark export, computed outside of Asta. Not generated by autodiscovery — manually verified.

## Claim

Not a single (spectrum, element) pair is caught by every identifier. This is the *diversity finding*: the 5 identifiers are genuinely heterogeneous in what they catch. Hybrid_union and the 2-of-3 majority mode (PR #164) are operating in a regime where consensus signals are weak but nonempty — every catch is carried by ≤4 of 5 members. Strengthening any individual member is more valuable than re-tuning the consensus rule on this corpus.

## What to do next

For the 2-of-3 majority mode (PR #164), examine which 2-of-3 subset is doing the work for each catch. If one identifier is consistently the swing vote, drop the other two from the consensus and you can lower compute cost without F1 loss.
