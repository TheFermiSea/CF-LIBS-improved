# Empirical Finding: 53/174 true (spectrum, element) pairs universally missed by all 5 identifiers

## Source

Direct analysis of the n=33 CF-LIBS benchmark export, computed outside of Asta. Not generated by autodiscovery — manually verified.

## Claim

Across the n=33 CF-LIBS benchmark, 53 out of 174 true (spectrum, element) pairs are missed by *every* one of {alias, comb, correlation, spectral_nnls, hybrid_union}. **Zero pairs are caught by all 5.** This is the direct empirical version of Asta exp #28 (super-union recall ceiling 0.667). The 0.85 literature ceiling is unreachable via algorithmic combination of current identifiers on this corpus.

## What to do next

Pick a single universally-missed pair, e.g. (vrabel_s039, Si), and run a focused physics audit: look at the spectrum, identify whether Si lines are above noise, check whether any identifier *attempts* to match (annotations.candidate_lines), and trace the rejection. If lines are present but below threshold, that's a threshold problem (PR #159 / #166 territory). If lines are absent or self-absorbed, that's a physical-modeling problem (PR #168 territory).
