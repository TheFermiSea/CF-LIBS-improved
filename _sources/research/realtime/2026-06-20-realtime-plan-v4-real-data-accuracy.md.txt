## ONE-LINE REAL-DATA VERDICT

**The 354 us structured-GN operating point is a genuine real-time win but it is NOT within usable CF-LIBS
composition accuracy on real data — real-matrix RMSE is ~0.12-0.17 number-fraction on ChemCam and ~20-26 wt%
on SuperCam (4x-10x worse than the 1-3 wt% CF-LIBS norm and ~2x worse than the 4-s legacy solver), and the
controlled round-trip proves the binding error is ATOMIC-DATA mismatch (~0.17 RMSE from the NIST/Kurucz line
list alone), not the inversion algorithm (floor ~0) — so better atomic data, not a faster solver, is the next
build step.**

## 0. Executive summary

v3 answered "can we do CF-LIBS composition in <1 ms?" and said **yes** (structured Jacobian, K=1, 354-408 us).
v4 answers the harder, real question the user actually cares about — **"is that fast number any GOOD on real
spectra, and would better atomic data fix it?"** — and the answer is sobering but clean:

1. **Real-data accuracy: NOT usable as a calibrated quant yet.** Per dataset, the deployed structured-GN
   single-shot solver lands at:
   - **Synthetic test suites (mass-frac):** basalt 0.097, carbonate 0.118, light 0.082, steel 0.165.
   - **Real ChemCam-family (mass-frac):** bhvo2_chemcam 0.122, chemcam_calib 0.158-0.164, csa_planetary
     0.163-0.166.
   - **SuperCam real (wt%):** labcal 19.9-26.3 wt%, scct 21.2-26.0 wt% RMSE.
   The published CF-LIBS norm for major-element quant is **1-3 wt% (~0.01-0.03 mass-frac)** on calibration
   standards; "good for a real matrix" is a few wt% on majors, looser on geology
   (`rtval_grounding.md` §2). Our fast solver is **4x-10x outside that** on every real dataset. It is a
   real-time *approximation* that returns plausible-but-uncalibrated composition, not a quant replacement.

2. **NIST-ASD vs Kurucz verdict (the user's core question): a faster solver does not move the needle; a
   better LINE LIST does — but only where the data is observable.** Swapping the atomic-data backend:
   - **Synthetic:** negligible (<1e-3 mass-frac either way) — forward and inverse share the same DB, so the
     line-list choice cancels.
   - **Real ChemCam:** Kurucz helps 2 of 3 matrices by **~0.004-0.006 mass-frac (~3-4% relative)**, with one
     small regression (csa_planetary +0.0033). Real but second-order at this overall error level.
   - **Real SuperCam:** Kurucz cuts RMSE by **~5-6 wt%** (labcal 26.3->19.9, scct 26.0->21.2) — a large,
     consistent, real improvement — yet **still ~20-22 wt%**, far outside usable.
   - **VALD: NOT TESTED** (gated, no autonomous access). This is an open blocker, not a negative result.
   The honest read: **atomic-data quality is the dominant remediable systematic** (confirmed independently
   by the controlled round-trip, item 3), and a more complete/accurate line list does reduce RMSE measurably
   on real data — most where the matrix is line-rich (SuperCam) — but no line-list swap alone gets us into
   CF-LIBS quant territory at this operating point.

3. **Algorithm-floor vs atomic-data decomposition (controlled round-trip, the cleanest evidence):**
   using a pure-linear NNLS separator (`S = B(T,ne)·comp` exactly linear in composition, B *is* the atomic
   data) at exact T/ne with zero noise:
   - **Algorithm floor (same line list, kurucz->kurucz): RMSE = 2.9e-6 (~0 wt%).** The forward+inverse close
     exactly. The inversion math is essentially error-free.
   - **Atomic-data mismatch (cross line list, kurucz->nist, still clean + exact T/ne): RMSE = 0.171
     number-frac (~17 wt%).** Per matrix: basalt +0.234 (worst), steel +0.170, carbonate +0.162, light
     +0.119. **The line-list choice alone injects ~0.17 RMSE with the algorithm contributing essentially
     nothing.**
   This is the decisive finding: **the binding error source for CF-LIBS quant here is atomic-data quality,
   not the inversion algorithm.** Optimizing the solver further (faster, more iterations, VarPro) cannot
   close a gap that is fundamentally in the line data.

4. **Legacy-vs-fast cross-check (real spectra):** the seconds-slow legacy iterative CF-LIBS
   (`cflibs.inversion.pipeline.run_pipeline`, detect->ID->Stark-ne->iterative closure) is **more accurate on
   4 of 5 real cases** (median RMSE 0.076 vs 0.151, ~2x better; 3-6x on well-behaved ChemCam shots) but
   **~2000x slower** (median 4.13 s vs ~2.0 ms at full 5860-ch window). The fast method **wins only on the
   22-element CSA andesite**, where the legacy detect/ID/closure pipeline collapsed (RMSE 0.366) and the GN
   degraded gracefully (0.151). So the fast solver trades ~2x worse accuracy for ~2000x speed, and is more
   robust to element-count stress.

5. **Honest latency correction to v3.** The v3 "354 us at ROI=2000" headline is from the *synthetic varpro
   sweep* (a leaner forward). On the **real ExoJAX Voigt path** the dense Voigt cost scales with grid size,
   so sub-ms is reached only at a **~250-500 ch LIBS ROI** (min 407 us at 250 ch); at **1500 ch the
   structured-GN is ~1.21 ms median**, and at the **full 5860-ch ChemCam window it is ~2.0 ms**, and at the
   **7933-ch SuperCam window K=1 is ~7.6 ms** (K=0 NNLS warm path ~0.62 ms). **The "<1 ms" claim is true only
   at small ROI; it is NOT true at full real-instrument resolution.** This does not change the v3 method
   choice (structured K=1 still dominates), but it tightens what we may claim.

6. **Robustness is excellent and is the one unambiguous strength.** Across 80 adversarial synthetic runs (6
   decades of SNR, tau up to 10, missing/confounder elements, K=1..5, 25..1000 ch, extreme T/ne, bad
   warm-starts) the structured-GN **never produced a NaN**; comp_rmse is flat under heavy noise; it degrades
   gracefully on missing/confounder elements. The failure modes are bounded and understood (below).

**Bottom line for the user:** the fast pipeline is real, robust, and ~2000x faster than legacy, but it is a
real-time *triage/approximation* tool at its current accuracy, not a calibrated quant. **The single highest-
leverage next investment is atomic-data quality (NIST A/B-grade gf curation + VALD evaluation), not more
solver speed** — the controlled round-trip proves the algorithm is already at its floor.

### 1.2 Honest read

- **No dataset, real or synthetic, lands inside the usable CF-LIBS band at the deployed operating point.**
  Even the cleanest synthetic case (light, 0.082) is ~3-5x over the 0.01-0.03 expectation; real ChemCam is
  ~5-8x; SuperCam wt% is ~7-10x over the 1-3 wt% norm.
- **The synthetic-vs-real gap is small** (synthetic 0.08-0.17 vs real ChemCam 0.12-0.17): this is itself
  diagnostic — it tells us the dominant error is **not** preprocessing/noise/real-spectrum messiness (which
  would blow up real far more than synthetic). The error is structural, and §3 localizes it to atomic data +
  the simplex composition step. The adversarial sweep corroborates: comp_rmse is flat from 50000 down to 1
  peak-count (`rtval_adversarial.md` §noise) — noise is not the binding axis.
- **Geological caveat applies but does not rescue it.** `rtval_grounding.md` §2 warns geology should carry
  looser thresholds than alloys; even with that latitude, 0.12-0.17 mass-frac and 20-26 wt% are above
  "semi-quantitative useful," and SuperCam per-element wt% (below) shows the alkali/Si columns are the worst.
- **SuperCam per-element structure** (`rtval_supercam_k0_summary.json`): the wt% RMSE is dominated by
  **Si (28-49 wt%), Na (43-55 wt%), K (12-50 wt%)** — i.e. the high-Z-abundance major (Si) and the
  weak/edge alkali lines (Na, K) — while **Ti is excellent (~1 wt%)** and Fe/Al/Mg/Mn are mid (8-24 wt%).
  This is exactly the "weak-emitter / large-dynamic-range" ill-posedness flagged in
  `SYNTH_RTVAL_FINDINGS.md` (Na/K alkali lines weak in 240-850 nm; basis-column-norm dynamic range ~1e6).

**Verdict (1):** **The 354 us operating point is NOT within usable CF-LIBS composition accuracy on real
data.** It is a robust real-time approximation (plausible composition, no NaNs, ~2000x faster than legacy),
appropriate for triage/screening/onboard-prioritization, but it must not be reported as calibrated quant.

## 2. NIST-ASD vs KURUCZ vs VALD VERDICT — does better atomic data move composition RMSE? (user core Q)

### 2.1 The measured deltas (NIST vs Kurucz), per regime

| Regime | dataset(s) | Kurucz->NIST delta (RMSE) | Interpretation |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Synthetic (same-DB fwd/inv) | basalt/carbonate/light/steel | <1e-3 mass-frac (both signs) | line-list cancels; near-zero |
| Real ChemCam | bhvo2_chemcam | Kurucz better by 0.0042 (~3.4% rel) | small, real |
| Real ChemCam | chemcam_calib | Kurucz better by 0.0060 (~3.7% rel) | small, real |
| Real ChemCam | csa_planetary | Kurucz **worse** by 0.0033 | small regression |
| Real SuperCam | supercam_labcal | Kurucz better by **6.42 wt%** | large, real |
| Real SuperCam | supercam_scct | Kurucz better by **4.86 wt%** | large, real |

### 2.2 Where better atomic data moves RMSE, and by how much

- **It moves RMSE most on line-rich real spectra (SuperCam): ~5-6 wt% reduction** switching NIST->Kurucz.
  Mechanistically consistent with `rtval_grounding.md` §1: Kurucz is far more *complete* (LIN/POS ratio ~370;
  330M iron-group lines), so on a broad real spectrum it back-fills weak/high-excitation lines NIST simply
  lacks, improving the Boltzmann/Saha intensity fit. NIST is more *accurate per line* but *incomplete*, and
  on a dense real spectrum completeness wins the aggregate fit.
- **On ChemCam it moves RMSE only ~0.004-0.006 mass-frac (~3-4% rel)**, sometimes the wrong way
  (csa_planetary) — at this regime the overall error (~0.16) swamps the line-list delta, so the choice is
  near-neutral.
- **On synthetic it does not move RMSE at all** (<1e-3): a same-DB round-trip is insensitive to *which* DB
  by construction — which is precisely why the *controlled* cross-DB round-trip (§3) is the right instrument
  to isolate the atomic-data effect.

### 2.3 Direction-of-effect resolution (important nuance)

There is an apparent tension: in the *operational* corpus (§2.1) Kurucz beats NIST on real data, but in the
*controlled clean* round-trip (§3) switching truth=Kurucz -> inv=NIST *adds* 0.171 RMSE. These are
consistent and measure different things:
- **§3 (controlled):** truth is *generated* with Kurucz, so kurucz->kurucz is tautologically exact and
  kurucz->nist measures pure *line-list disagreement* (a lower bound on atomic-data sensitivity, not a
  statement that Kurucz is "true").
- **§2.1 (operational, real spectra):** truth is the *certificate/ICP*, independent of both DBs, so the
  comparison is fair — and there Kurucz's **completeness** helps the fit on dense real spectra.
The reconciled conclusion: **atomic-data choice is a first-order lever (0.005-6 wt% on real data, ~0.17 in
the controlled cross-DB test), its sign/magnitude is matrix- and completeness-dependent, and on real
line-rich spectra the more complete list (Kurucz) currently wins the aggregate fit** — while per-line
accuracy (NIST A/B grades) is what would let a *curated* list beat both (the §5 recommendation).

### 2.4 VALD — open blocker

**VALD was NOT tested in either arm** (no VALD rows in any CSV; controlled round-trip explicitly skipped it:
"VALD SKIPPED (gated, no autonomous access)"). `rtval_grounding.md` §1 positions VALD3 as the **right
default for this use case**: more complete than NIST, more accurate than raw Kurucz (it merges
critically-evaluated per-species lab/theory gf — Fe II Raassen & Uylings, Ti II Pickering, etc. — and
back-fills the rest from Kurucz). The literature expectation is that VALD3 would **combine** the SuperCam
completeness win (Kurucz-like) with better per-line accuracy than Kurucz — i.e. plausibly the best of the
three — **but this is an untested hypothesis, flagged as the top atomic-data follow-up.**

**Verdict (2):** **Better atomic data does measurably move real-data composition RMSE** — most on line-rich
SuperCam (~5-6 wt% NIST->Kurucz), modestly on ChemCam (~3-4% rel), not at all on same-DB synthetic — and the
controlled test proves atomic-data mismatch is the *dominant* error term (~0.17 RMSE). But **no single
line-list swap tested gets us into usable CF-LIBS quant**; the path forward is a **curated A/B-grade +
completeness list (NIST-graded gf + VALD/Kurucz back-fill with a gf-grade column), with VALD evaluated**, not
a binary NIST-vs-Kurucz pick.

## 3. ALGORITHM-FLOOR vs ATOMIC-DATA-MISMATCH DECOMPOSITION (controlled round-trip)

Source: `/tmp/rtval_local/SYNTH_RTVAL_FINDINGS.md` + `/tmp/rtval_synth.csv` (1216 rows), all numbers
re-derived this session. Design: truth generated from the most-complete Kurucz bundle (184k-line source),
known (T,ne,comp), realistic Poisson+Gaussian noise; inverted with a **clean linear NNLS separator**
(`S = B(T,ne)·comp` exactly linear in composition; B *is* the atomic data) so composition error is not
confounded by the simplex/GN nonlinearity. Element-level physics (U(T), IP, mass) identical across backends —
only the line list differs.

| Stage | kurucz->kurucz (same DB) | kurucz->nist (cross DB) | What it isolates |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| **clean @ true T,ne** | **2.9e-6 (~0)** | **0.171 +/- 0.060** | algo floor vs **pure atomic-data mismatch** |
| noisy @ true T,ne | 0.208 | 0.214 | + noise (weak-emitter floor) |
| noisy @ GN-recovered T,ne | 0.200 | 0.209 | + plasma-param error (operational) |

Per-matrix atomic-data delta (clean, cross-DB): **basalt +0.234 (worst), steel +0.170, carbonate +0.162,
light +0.119.**

### Reading

- **The algorithm floor is essentially zero (2.9e-6).** A same-line-list clean round-trip closes exactly:
  the inversion math (Saha-Boltzmann + closure/NNLS) is not the problem. **There is no meaningful accuracy
  to be won by improving the solver.**
- **Atomic-data mismatch alone is ~0.171 RMSE (~17 wt%)** at zero noise and exact plasma params. The line
  list is the binding error source by a wide margin over the algorithm.
- **The noisy operational floor (~0.20 at both backends) is dominated by near-unobservable candidate
  elements** (Na/K alkali lines weak/edge in 240-850 nm; basis-column-norm dynamic range ~1e6). At realistic
  SNR this *shared* noise floor MASKS the atomic-data delta (NIST and Kurucz both ~0.20-0.21 noisy) — which
  is exactly why the operational corpus (§2.1) shows only small NIST-vs-Kurucz deltas on ChemCam: the
  weak-emitter ill-posedness, not the line list, sets the noisy ceiling there. This is **real CF-LIBS
  physics, not a bug.**

**Verdict (3):** **algorithm error ~0; atomic-data mismatch ~0.17; weak-emitter/noise floor ~0.20.** The
error budget is, in order: (1) weak-emitter ill-posedness on a wide window (Na/K/Si dynamic range), (2)
atomic-data line-list quality, (3) plasma-param (ne) error — with the **inversion algorithm contributing
essentially nothing.** Spend effort on line data + line selection (downweight weak emitters / restrict ROI
to observable diagnostics), not on the solver.

## 5. HONEST BLOCKERS, HARNESS CAVEATS, AND NEXT BUILD STEP

### 5.1 Blockers (honest)

1. **VALD untested (gated, no autonomous access).** The literature-favored default (VALD3) is the one
   atomic-data option never evaluated. This is the **#1 open question** behind the user's core ask — we have
   NIST-vs-Kurucz only. Needs a VALD3 extract (gated download) into the bundle builder.
2. **"<1 ms" is ROI-conditional, not full-resolution.** On the real ExoJAX Voigt path: sub-ms only at
   ~250-500 ch (min 407 us); **1500 ch = ~1.21 ms, 5860 ch (full ChemCam) = ~2.0 ms, 7933 ch (SuperCam) K=1
   = ~7.6 ms.** The v3 "354 us at ROI=2000" is a synthetic-sweep figure on a leaner forward; do not claim
   <1 ms at full real-instrument resolution. (The K=0 NNLS warm path is ~0.62 ms at 7933 ch — sub-ms but
   that is the warm-start, not the GN step.)
3. **Real-data composition is diagnostic, not calibrated.** Real comparisons are number-fraction(recovered)
   vs wt%(certificate) over *shared elements, renormalized* — a self-consistency diagnostic, not a true
   calibrated CF-LIBS quant with full element set + self-absorption correction + curated gf. Absolute real
   RMSE will differ from a production-calibrated run; the *relative* (NIST vs Kurucz, GN vs legacy, fast vs
   floor) comparisons are the trustworthy outputs.
4. **Harness/forward differences across arms.** Three forward engines appear: the synthetic varpro sweep
   (v3 latency, leaner forward), the ExoJAX-grade reference forward (real + adversarial + controlled), and
   the SuperCam harness. Latency numbers are **not** cross-comparable between the varpro sweep and the
   ExoJAX path; accuracy numbers (RMSE) are comparable within an arm. No silent fallback was hit (GPU
   verified `CudaDevice(id=0)` in all ExoJAX runs), but **the controlled round-trip uses an NNLS separator,
   not the deployed GN**, deliberately — the GN's simplex composition step is its known weak spot, so the
   algo/atomic split is cleaner with NNLS. The deployed-GN real numbers (§1) carry the GN simplex penalty on
   top of the §3 atomic-data floor.
5. **Self-absorption must stay upstream.** `use_self_absorption=True` inside the GN basis **breaks**
   (ne rails to floor, comp_rmse 0.25-0.29, T diverges at K=3). Keep the GN strictly optically-thin; handle
   SA via line selection / CDSB upstream (adversarial §3). This is a hard constraint on the solver design.
6. **ne is unidentifiable from a flat prior** (ne_rel ~0.3-0.5 nominal, ~1.0 at ne<=1e16). Must seed ne from
   a Stark-width diagnostic (legacy does `measure_stark_ne`); the real-time path needs this seed or it
   carries ne error into composition.

### 5.2 Next build step (re-pointed from v3 — the lever moved from SPEED to DATA)

v3's next step was "implement + oracle-test the structured Jacobian." That latency work stands and is largely
validated. **But the controlled round-trip (§3) proves the algorithm is already at its floor, so the next
*accuracy* build step is atomic-data, not solver:**

**STEP A (do first) — Curated, gf-graded atomic-line bundle + VALD evaluation.**
1. Extend the bundle builder (`/tmp/rtval_local/build_atomic_bundle.py` / `cflibs/atomic`) to carry a
   **per-line gf-grade column** (NIST AAA/AA/A/B/C/D/E) as a first-class field
   (`rtval_grounding.md` §1 recommendation: "a gf-grade column should be a first-class input to line
   selection").
2. Build three comparable bundles over the SAME line set: **NIST-A/B-only**, **Kurucz-complete**, and
   **VALD3** (gated download — unblock #1). Re-run the controlled cross-DB round-trip (`synth_rtval.py`) and
   the real corpus (`rtval_all` aggregation) across all three. **Acceptance:** does VALD3 beat both on real
   SuperCam/ChemCam RMSE, and does NIST-A/B-only (accuracy-capped) beat raw Kurucz on the *controlled* test?
3. **Line-selection gate:** downweight/exclude Kurucz-back-filled (no NIST grade) and weak-emitter lines
   (basis-column-norm threshold) from the composition fit — directly attacks the §3 weak-emitter ~0.20
   noisy floor (Na/K/Si). Re-measure real RMSE.

**STEP B (parallel) — keep the v3 solver work, but re-scope its claim.**
- Ship the structured K=1 GN as the **real-time triage/warm-start** path with the honest claim: **sub-ms at
  ~250-500 ch ROI, ~2 ms at full window, plausible (not calibrated) composition, ~2000x faster than legacy,
