Empirical Finding: 49/53 universal misses are on vrabel2020_soil_benchmark (rp=30000)

Source

Direct analysis of the n=33 CF-LIBS benchmark export, computed outside of Asta. Not generated by autodiscovery — manually verified.

Claim

Of the 53 universally-missed pairs, 49 (92.5%) are from the Vrabel soil benchmark with rp=30000. Only 4 are on BHVO-2 (rp=9433). The ‘universal miss’ signal is almost entirely a Vrabel-corpus signal. Combined with Asta exp #20 (comb performance correlates negatively with rp) and exp #26 (alias failures have higher rp), this suggests that the rp=30000 regime is genuinely harder for the line-matching family — finer resolving power produces more candidate peaks, which overloads the matching heuristic.

What to do next

Two paths: (a) Treat rp as a stratification variable in the F1 verifier — report per-rp-bucket F1 (PR #169 is partway there). (b) Add a rp-aware threshold for comb/correlation that increases min_correlation as rp grows. This is a tunable knob already; the experiment is to scan the threshold-vs-rp surface on Vrabel alone and pick the optimal coupling.
